# M0.1c — the M0.1 workload, finally on Vire IR (persistent live set)

*The actual gate-closer. `M0.1b-VIRE-MEASUREMENT.md` turned the wrong knob: "churn"
varied the collection **frequency** over a **small** live set — that is linear,
because every scan is cheap. The M0.1 pathology is collection over a **large,
persistent, live, shared, mutating** set: cost ≈ (collections) × (live-set size).
This measurement reproduces EXACTLY that condition, for the first time on
Vire-generated IR.*

## Workload (`benchmarks/vire-m0/pagerank.vr`)
Doubly linked ring of N nodes: each node is referenced by two others
(**refcount 2**, >1 → cycle candidate) and the whole ring is **one cycle**.
`build_ring` returns it → escapes → **heap** (IR: 2× `New`, 0× `StackNew` —
confirmed heap-resident). `pagerank` keeps it **live across 40 iterations** and
mutates each node in place (`rank = (prev.rank+next.rank)/2+1`). This is the
shared/cyclic/mutating/persistently-live set from M0.1.

Matched Rust indices baseline (`pagerank_rust.rs`): the same topology as `Vec`
indices (no RC, no collector). **Both produce bit-identical output** (N=2000 →
82040); the workload is equivalent.

## Three-way (best-of-3, `-O2` both sides, one machine)

| N | Vire collector-ON | Vire collector-OFF (`--no-cycles`) | Rust indices | ON/Rust | OFF/Rust |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 16 000 | 0.0092 s | 0.0046 s | 0.0027 s | **3.5×** | 1.7× |
| 64 000 | 0.0308 s | 0.0153 s | 0.0068 s | **4.5×** | 2.3× |
| 256 000 | 0.1144 s | 0.0501 s | 0.0267 s | **4.3×** | 1.9× |

## Verdict: the M0.1 blowup does NOT reproduce on Vire IR
- **Collector-ON is ~3.5–4.5× Rust and FLAT** across 16× growth in size (3.5× →
  4.3×). That is **linear**, not O(n²). At 256k it runs in 0.11 s;
  **M0.1 timed out at 100k (>60 s).**
- **Precise scope of the word "artifact" (two separate claims, not one):**
  1. *The O(n²) scaling* was a collector-implementation artifact
     (pre-adaptive-threshold), not a structural necessity — that is refuted and
     stays refuted (the adaptive-threshold fix lives in the shared runtime and
     carries over onto Vire IR).
  2. *The constant RC+collector factor* (~4× with collector, ~2× RC-only) is
     **real and survives** — it did not explode, but it also did not vanish.
  The honest one-liner formula: **"the O(n²) was an artifact; the constant
  RC+collector tax is real and ~4×."** "M0 does not reproduce" does NOT mean
  "RC is now free" — precisely what the oracle measurement below shows
  quantitatively.
- **Consequence for prioritization:** the capsule arena is thereby an
  **optimization** (~4× → closer to Rust), **not a rescue** from O(n²). Its
  urgency drops sharply — it is decided on a *measured* number, not on M0's memory
  of one.

## Sharpest stress: the NAIVE thrash form collapses onto the stack
The ON/OFF ratio above is ~constant (~2×), not growing → the collector does
**per-release buffer bookkeeping**, not repeated full scans (the adaptive
threshold 2×live does not fire as long as candidates ≤ live set). To force the
sharper M0 form — transient cycle garbage > 2×live IN ADDITION to the large live
set → forced mid-run rescans — `benchmarks/vire-m0/thrash.vr` creates, per
inner-loop step, a small `a<->b` cycle across a function boundary
(`junk() -> Node`, returns the cycle) that dies immediately.

**Result: this garbage never reaches the collector** — the escape analysis (after
inlining + loop stack, cycle-safe) **stack-allocates** it (IR: 2× `StackNew`; at
N=4000 only **4000** heap objects, not the 160 000 `junk` calls). Timing linear
(16k/64k/256k → 0.0116/0.0426/0.1550 s, all 0 live).

**What this proves — and what it does NOT (deliberately kept narrow):** The
*naive* thrash form (garbage that is created and dies within a function boundary)
is exactly the escape analysis's home turf and collapses onto the stack. That does
NOT mean "the thrash condition is unconstructable." The actual M0 killer is not
transient garbage but *persistent* shared-mutable state that simultaneously
**escapes AND is cyclic AND churns** — precisely what the escape analysis by
definition CANNOT stack. T12 shows: the *simple* adversarial form goes onto the
stack. It does not show that the *hard* form is impossible — precisely the hard
form is what region inference exists for. So the safe claim is: **"the naive
thrash form is caught by the escape analysis; a form that beats it and churns
cyclically is region-inference territory"** — not "unconstructable."
- **~4× Rust is not parity.** RC alone (OFF) costs ~2×, the collector adds ~2× on
  top. That is the honest Vire tax on the shared/cyclic path — better than 108×,
  but not Rust-level (the oracle measurement below says how far the tax is
  provably removable).
- One machine, best-of-3, `-O2`. No no_std/threads case.

## Oracle: the ceiling of region inference (before a single line of it is built)
Before sinking a quarter into region/borrow inference, one measures its ceiling —
just as M0 gated the frontend. The ring is **loop-stable**: built once, then
mutated *in place* across 40 iterations, **no topology change** (`next`/`prev` are
never reassigned after the build). An ideal region inference recognizes that and
elides ALL RC ops on this stable set. Since the transient garbage is stacked
anyway, the stable set here IS the whole heap → the oracle is "RC globally off"
(`vire build --no-rc`: retain/release no-op; unsound, leaks — ceiling timing
only).

Four-way (best-of-5, `-O2`):

| N | collector-ON | RC-only (`--no-cycles`) | **RC-OFF oracle (`--no-rc`)** | Rust |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 16 000 | 3.3× | 1.6× | **1.4×** | 1× |
| 64 000 | 4.5× | 2.0× | **1.1×** | 1× |
| 256 000 | 5.1× | 2.2× | **1.1×** | 1× |

**The ceiling is parity.** RC-OFF converges at scale to **~1.1× Rust** (the 1.4× at
16k is fixed cost that vanishes at larger N). This answers the load-bearing
question of the whole bet:
- **RC is the dominant cost block** (RC-only 2.2× → RC-OFF 1.1× at 256k: the RC
  doubles the time). Removing it by proof reaches all the way to Rust — NOT just
  ~2.5×. Region inference thereby has a **real, measured target** (parity), not a
  hope. The cost staircase at 256k: **parity 1.1× → +RC 2.2× → +collector 5.1×.**
- Notable: the Rust baseline uses flat `Vec` indices (cache-friendly), Vire chases
  heap pointers — and RC-free still lands at 1.1×. The pointer-chasing disadvantage
  is hidden under the work; the RC tax is the real difference, not the layout.

**Limit of the oracle (honest):** This is the ceiling for THIS workload, where the
stable set = the whole heap. It says "if the inference proves the loop stability,
parity is reachable" — it does NOT say the inference *can* prove it. Proving it
(built-once + no topology mutation + shared-but-stable, without annotations) is the
actual research. But the payoff for it is now **measured: parity**, not
remembered.

## Curve, not endpoint: 100% elision is not the decisive number
The `--no-rc` oracle measures only the **100% point**. Real inference elides a
*subset*, and the costs are not evenly distributed. Two measurements sharpen this
(N=256k, collector off, best-of-5), and the first inverts the picture:

**(A) RC cost is ~90% call machinery, ~10% arithmetic.** If one marks a fraction of
the objects immortal (`FASTLLVM_RC_ELIDE_PCT`, retain/release *body* no-op, but the
*call* remains: load + branch `if refcount<0`), it drops from 2.30× (0%) only to
2.07× (100%). Only removing the *calls* (`--no-rc`) brings 2.07× → 1.1×. So: the
refcount arithmetic costs ~10%, the call/branch/load machinery ~90%.
**Consequence:** a runtime region *flag* (marking an object) fetches almost
nothing; parity demands **compile-time elimination of the call sites** — precisely
what region inference does (and what the backend borrow-slot pass already does for
`this`).

**(B) The call-site curve is STEEP — the cost sits in a minority of hot sites.** If
one deletes a fraction of the `jrt_retain/release` calls directly in the LLVM:

| Call sites elided | ×Rust |
|---|---|
| 0% | 2.01× |
| 25% | 1.92× |
| **50%** | **1.17×** |
| 75% | 1.19× |
| 100% | 1.17× |

Almost all of the savings comes in the 25→50% band; before and after, flat. Reason:
the traversal calls (run N×40 times) carry ~everything, the build/sum calls (N times
each) carry almost nothing. This is the predicted "the last sites carry the lion's
share" shape — **but here it falls in favor of the inference:** on loop-stable code
the hot sites (traversal of a *topology-invariant* structure) are exactly the
**provable** ones. Even ~50% well-targeted (= the hot) elision reaches parity; the
inference need not be complete, only hit the hot loop.

**Double-edged, honest:** The concentration helps ONLY if the hot subset is the
provable one. On **topology-mutating** code (dynamic PageRank with node
join/leave, a tree-rebuilding parser) the hot sites are precisely those where
"built-once + invariant" does NOT hold → inference does not grip there, it stays RC
(~2×) or `capsule` arena. My elision is moreover *position-based* (file order), so
the exact knee position is an artifact; only the *concentration* (cost in a few hot
sites) is robust, not the exact 50% number.

**Scope of the 1.1×/parity claim (against over-reading):** Parity is reachable on
**loop-stable** graphs (built-once, mutated in place, topology invariant). The ring
is the *friendliest* such case. Topology-mutating graphs stay RC (~2×) or arena —
for them the parity ceiling does NOT hold. "1.1× on the ring" is not "1.1× in
general," just as "M0 does not reproduce" was not "RC is free."

## Gate status: answered — and the next cost driver is named AND measured
The M0 gate is answered in the "does not reproduce" direction: O(n²) is an artifact
(gone), the constant RC+collector tax is real (~4×), and its provably-removable
ceiling is **parity (~1.1×)**. Therefore:
1. **capsule arena: optional optimization**, not a gate blocker. Only as a perf
   feature with M0.2 (pure form vs Rust arena), buildable at any time when a hot
   capsule workload shows up.
2. **Region/borrow inference on SSA now has a proven, SHARPENED target.** The
   central bet — "the solver proves the RC away, as it proves bounds checks away" —
   is reachable to parity on loop-stable code, BUT the curve (above) says exactly
   what it MUST be able to do: not "elide all RC," but **eliminate the compile-time
   call sites of the hot loop** (object flagging fetches ~10%, the concentration
   sits in the traversal loop). On loop-stable code, hot sites = provable sites →
   ~50% targeted elision suffices. On topology-mutating code this falls away (stays
   RC/arena). The backend borrow-slot pass already does this for `this`; the
   research is extending it to shared-but-loop-stable objects without annotations.
   **Months-piece, human directional decision**, not momentum.
3. Hardening: no_std/threads (atomic RC — more expensive, contended), more
   topologies, explicitly constructing the hard thrash form
   (escaping+cyclic+churning).
